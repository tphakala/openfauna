# Attribution and Data Sources

OpenFauna is an open-source biological metadata and translation dictionary built for the global bioacoustics and environmental monitoring community. 

This project relies on the incredible work of researchers and open-source contributors around the world. We deeply respect their terms of use and encourage all users of OpenFauna to provide similar attribution in their derived works.

## Baseline Translation Data (BirdNET)

The initial baseline of multi-language common name translations (for over 13,000 species across 30 languages) was bootstrapped directly from the **BirdNET+ V3.0** developer preview taxonomy dataset.

We extend our deepest gratitude to the BirdNET team for releasing their taxonomy data under the Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International (CC BY-SA 4.0) license.

**BirdNET V3.0 Contributors & Institutions:**
- K. Lisa Yang Center for Conservation Bioacoustics (Cornell University)
- Chemnitz University of Technology
- Museum für Naturkunde Berlin
- Stefan Kahl and the extended BirdNET research team

**References:**
- Kahl et al. (2021): *BirdNET: A deep learning solution for avian diversity monitoring.*
- Lasseck (2018): *Audio-based Bird Species Identification with Deep Convolutional Neural Networks.*

*If you use OpenFauna in academic or commercial applications, you must include attribution to the original BirdNET project as required by their CC BY-SA 4.0 license.*

## Additional Data Sources

As OpenFauna expands to include family classifications, Wikipedia URLs, and iNaturalist identifiers, those data points are sourced from public, open-access databases.

- **Wikipedia**: Article URLs and text snippets are sourced from Wikipedia and are licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike License (CC BY-SA).
- **iNaturalist**: Species identifiers and taxonomies map to the iNaturalist open taxonomy. Any thumbnails or photos retrieved using these IDs must be individually verified for open-source (CC) licensing on the iNaturalist platform.
